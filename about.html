About Laws.Africa

We believe that the traditional approach to promoting free access to the law requires a fundamental shift. Firstly, putting documents online does not enable value creation and is not enough. Secondly, sourcing, consolidating and providing open access to the law sustainably requires a new business model for collaboration and partnerships.

Our Goals

  1. Improve access to the law, stimulate innovation, and lead by example, by
  2. creating a machine-friendly, openly licensed legislation commons, through
  3. building a mutually beneficial relationship with a volunteer community, that is
  4. sustainable through licensing and charging for value-added services and expertise.

Our Principles

We believe this new model of access to legislation is based on the following principles:

  1. Access to the law directly and indirectly enables access to justice.
  2. A legislation commons improves access to the law for everyone.
  3. A legislation commons promotes justice sector innovation by lowering barriers to entry and reducing undifferentiated heavy lifting.
  4. Such a commons must be machine-friendly and, ultimately, openly licensed.
  5. Such a commons can only be sustainably built in mutual collaboration with those who benefit from it.
  6. Such a commons can be built sustainably through appropriate licensing and the offering of value-added features and services, without limiting the usefulness of the open commons.
