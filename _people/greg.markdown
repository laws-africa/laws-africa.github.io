Greg is a co-founder of Laws.Africa, where he leads the development of legal information systems that provide free, structured access to legislation, case law, and government gazettes across multiple African countries.

He is a software engineer focused on building practical, reliable systems that improve how legal information is created, maintained, and used. His work increasingly explores the role of AI in legal information systems, with an emphasis on real-world usefulness rather than novelty.

Greg’s work sits at the intersection of public infrastructure, legal systems, and sustainable business models for open access to law. He works closely with governments, legal professionals, and partner organisations to strengthen access to legal information across Africa.

Before founding Laws.Africa, Greg worked at OpenUp, a civic technology organisation, and Amazon Web Services (AWS). He holds an MSc in Computer Science from the University of British Columbia.
